The IHTSDO Terminology Tooling

v.2013

Two major components make up the core of IHTSDO tooling

The IHTSDO Workbench
is a terminology authoring tool primarily used to author & manage
the content of SNOMED CT

The IHTSDO SNOMED CT Release Process
is a 'back-end' process run twice a year to release the international edition of SNOMED CT

Who is using the Workbench within the IHTSDO?

The IHTSDO International authoring team

The Swedish National Resource Centre *
The Danish National Resource Centre *

And outside...

The National Library of Medicines (NLM) for the USA

The National Health Service (NHS) for the UK

THE National E-health Transition Authority (NEHTA) for Australia

... plus more 'local' organizations including the VA in the USA & Kaiser Permanente

What the Workbench isn't

Standalone, without a release 'back-end', it will not export RF2 format files or produce a release

No provision for RFC, the IHTSDO does this in a non-integrated online tool (SIRS)

On its own, the Workbench is not a tool to manage a national extension,
but has evolved for authoring the International Edition of SNOMED CT

The Workbench is..

It is a mature and stable SNOMED CT authoring tool

It is released in three versions:

  • International Edition
  • Members General Release Edition
  • Managed Service Edition(s)

It is a Java Swing desktop application
which is downloaded from the IHTSDO Collabnet site

It will run on most operating systems including MS Windows and Mac OSX

The relevant terminology, including history, refsets, and authoring users are bundled into the install package

It is used to author the International edition of SNOMED CT, and meets most collaborative terminology authoring requirements

The full source code for the tool is available under an Apache 2 open source license and is available to anyone.
